MODEL 1: Linear Regression for FPR Prediction
- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - 

Mathematical Form:
  log(FPR) = β₀ + β₁(m/n) + ε

Where:
  - FPR: False Positive Rate (outcome variable y₂)
  - m/n: Bits per element ratio (predictor x₁)
  - β₀: Intercept (baseline log(FPR) when m/n = 0)
  - β₁: Slope (change in log(FPR) per unit increase in m/n)
  - ε: Error term ~ N(0, σ²)

Estimation Method:
  Ordinary Least Squares (OLS)
  Minimizes: Σ(log(FPR_observed) - log(FPR_predicted))²

Assumptions:
  1. Linearity: log(FPR) is linear in m/n
  2. Independence: Observations are independent
  3. Homoscedasticity: Constant variance of residuals
  4. Normality: Residuals follow normal distribution

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - 

MODEL 2: Paired Samples for Latency Comparison
- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - 

Mathematical Form:
  D_i = Y_without_i - Y_with_i
  where D_i ~ N(μ_D, σ²_D)

Where:
  - Y_without: Latency without Bloom filter (ms)
  - Y_with: Latency with Bloom filter (ms)
  - D_i: Paired difference for request i
  - μ_D: Mean difference (expected latency reduction)

Estimation Method:
  Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) under normality
  μ̂_D = (1/n) Σ D_i
  σ̂²_D = (1/(n-1)) Σ (D_i - μ̂_D)²

Test Statistic:
  t = μ̂_D / (σ̂_D / √n)
  Follows t-distribution with (n-1) degrees of freedom

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - 

MODEL 3: Theoretical FPR Formula (Bloom, 1970)
- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - 

Mathematical Form:
  FPR = (1 - e^(-kn/m))^k

Where:
  - k: Number of hash functions
  - n: Number of inserted elements
  - m: Size of bit array

Optimal Parameters:
  k* = (m/n) × ln(2)  [minimizes FPR]
  m* = -n × ln(p) / (ln(2))²  [for target FPR = p]

Validation Method:
  Chi-square goodness-of-fit test comparing empirical vs theoretical
